    • Where I Call Home Clock (2025)

    Project Description

    The time, day, and weather information of two cities is updated every 5 minutes, provided via the OpenWeather API. The black & white colors of the e-ink screen represent nighttime and daytime in the cities. This data that fits neatly in a 2" screen is concise, but powerful predictors outlining what it's like in that city right now.

    Designed to be a useful desk clock providing a quick overview of the two cities that I'm currently involved with, this is also an autobiographical piece exploring what "home" means to me -- a state that is plural, transitory, and relational. The beads and cities can be switched out when there is a change of circumstances.

    Technology

    API communication, wifi, low power design

    Materials

    ESP-32, e-ink display, 9V battery, circuit, acrylic, letter beads
