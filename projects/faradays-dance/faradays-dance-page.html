    • Faraday's Dance (2025)

    Project Description

    A wearable device that captures freeform movement and transforms it into light. Powered solely by the kinetic energy harvested from the movement and lasting as much, it is a self-contained nod of appreciation to the beauty of ephemeral motion.

    Technology

    electromagnetism, wearable technology

    Materials

    copper coil, magnets, LED, circuit, velcro straps, 3D-printed plastic, plywood

    Collaborators

    Audrey Oh

    In Motion

    device worn on the wrist / arm movements inducing light / the produced light drawing

    The Components

    the generator using magnets and coil / the rectifier circuit / the complete circuit

    Room For Improvement

    For this device to be more integrated into daily movement, it needs to be made with softer materials. An version with soft circuits and a more fashionable adaptation is to be explored.
