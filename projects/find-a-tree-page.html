    • Find a tree, carve a hole (2025)

    Project Description

    Inspired by a scene in 'In the Mood for Love (2000)' where the protagonist carves a hole in a tree to whisper a secret into and seals it with mud, this is a recreation of that experience in a furniture-like adaptation. When the audience turns the handles on each side, the box opens up a hole where the secret can be kept. After the confession, one can turn back the handle to seal the box closed.

    This piece was featured in the [Lab from New York] exhibition, held at the National Science Museum (Daejeon, South Korea) from 1/6 to 1/11, 2026.

    Materials

    wood, 3D-printed plastic, stainless steel, LED

    Technology

    mechanism design, rapid prototyping using cardboard and 3D printing, exhibition design

    Process

    I started off sketching what my desired form and interaction would look like - and drafted many, many possible mechanisms.

  • To have a spacial visualization of the piece, I created a prototype using cardboard.
  • To see how people feel about different shapes of the moving discs, I created mock-ups collected feedback from 20+ people.

    • I tested out the mechanism again with cardboard, then modeling in CAD and 3D printing and iterating.

    As I made this piece in New York but had to transport it to Korea, I made the design modular for easy (dis)assembly.

    For the exhibition, I designed the experience and layout of the space, and fabricated elements needed. I made sure the viewer took time to understand the premise and have a tactile and personal experience with the piece. The canopy of wooden sticks combined with forest-smelling olfactory experience added to the mood.
